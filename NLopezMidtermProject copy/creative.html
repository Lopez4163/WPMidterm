Cali, Colombia

Cali, Colombia, known as the "Salsa Capital of the World," is a city where the rhythm of salsa music and dance is central to daily life and Colombian cultural identity. This lively heritage is matched by Cali’s unique cuisine, influenced by Afro-Colombian, indigenous, and Spanish traditions. Signature dishes like sancocho de gallina, a rich chicken stew with plantains, yuca, and corn, and pandebono, a beloved cheese bread, are staples that reflect the region’s agricultural bounty. Cali’s culinary and musical traditions together tell a story of a city that pulses with flavor and rhythm, deeply enriching Colombia's cultural landscape.

Jaen, Peru

Jaén, Peru, is a vibrant town nestled in the northern Amazon region, known for its tropical climate and rich cultural heritage. The area is famous for its coffee and cacao production, yielding some of the finest beans in Peru due to its fertile soil and ideal growing conditions. Beyond agriculture, Jaén is deeply rooted in indigenous traditions, with local festivals and celebrations honoring its diverse heritage. The cuisine here reflects a blend of highland and Amazonian flavors, with popular dishes like juane, a flavorful rice and chicken dish wrapped in jungle leaves, and tacacho con cuyi, mashed plantains served with traditional roasted guinea pig. These culinary specialties showcase Jaén’s unique place within Peru’s cultural mosaic.
